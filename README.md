# World Happiness Analysis Using Python

## Project Overview

The goal of this project is to understand which factors most strongly influence happiness scores globally. Using Python, the analysis includes:
- Data cleaning and preprocessing
- Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
- Visualization of trends and relationships
- Statistical insights on predictors of happiness

## Dataset
The dataset used in this analysis includes:
-Ladder score (happiness score)
-GDP per capita
-Social support
-Healthy life expectancy
-Freedom to make life choices
-Generosity
-Perceptions of corruption
-Source: World Happiness Report

## Key Findings
- Economic Wealth Matters: Countries with higher GDP per capita tend to have higher happiness scores. Economic prosperity is a strong predictor of well-being.
- Social Support & Health: Strong social networks and higher healthy life expectancy are strongly correlated with higher happiness.
- Freedom to Choose: Perceived freedom to make life choices is positively associated with happiness scores.
- Generosity & Governance: Higher generosity and lower corruption perception slightly improve happiness, although their impact is smaller than GDP, support, and health.
- Top vs. Bottom Countries: High-ranking countries on the happiness ladder are typically high-income nations with strong social and health infrastructure, while low-ranking countries often face challenges in these areas.
- Combined Factors: A mix of economic, social, health, and governance factors explains most of the variance in national happiness scores.

## Implications
- Economic growth alone is insufficient; social, health, and institutional factors play critical roles.
- Policymakers can improve national happiness by investing in social support systems, public health, freedom, and governance.
- Further analysis could explore longitudinal trends or additional factors like inequality, employment, and education.
